## Selecting an Investor

### Investor Sidebar

On the main arena page, you'll see a sidebar with available investors.

**Investor Information Displayed:**
- **Name** - The investor's full name
- **Role** - Their persona (e.g., "The Titan", "The Skeptic")
- **Region** - Geographic location
- **Check Size** - Investment range they typically write
- **Target Sector** - Industries they focus on

### How to Select

1. **Browse the list** of available investors
2. **Click on an investor** to view full details
3. **Click "Start Pitch Session"** to begin
4. The chat interface will open with the selected investor

### Investor Types

**The Titan**
- High-risk, high-reward investor
- Focuses on massive market opportunities
- Expects aggressive growth strategies
- Large check sizes ($5M - $25M)

**The Skeptic**
- Questions everything
- Demands strong proof of traction
- Conservative with capital
- Moderate check sizes ($2M - $10M)

**The Mentor**
- Supportive and coaching-oriented
- Values founder journey and learning
- Patient with business development
- Smaller to medium checks ($500K - $5M)

**The Analyst**
- Data-driven decision maker
- Requires detailed metrics
- Focuses on unit economics
- Medium to large checks ($3M - $15M)

## Pitching Your Startup

### Starting a Conversation

1. After selecting an investor, you'll see the chat interface
2. The investor will typically greet you first
3. Begin with your elevator pitch

### Chat Interface

**Components:**
- **Message Area** - Where conversation appears
- **Input Box** - Where you type your responses
- **Microphone Button** - For voice input (if supported)
- **Turn Counter** - Shows remaining conversation turns

### Text Input

**How to Send Messages:**
1. Type your message in the input box
2. Press Enter or click Send button
3. Wait for AI response (typically 2-10 seconds)

**Message Tips:**
- Be clear and concise
- Answer questions directly
- Provide specific numbers when asked
- Don't write extremely long messages

### Voice Input

**Using Voice Input:**
1. Click the microphone icon
2. Allow microphone permissions (if first time)
3. Speak your message clearly
4. Text will appear in input box
5. Review and click Send

**Voice Input Tips:**
- Speak clearly and at moderate pace
- Minimize background noise
- Review text before sending (voice recognition isn't perfect)
- Use headphones for better accuracy

**Supported Languages:**
- English (US, UK, Australia)
- Spanish
- French
- German
- And others (browser-dependent)

## Understanding Investor Responses

### Response Styles

Each investor has a unique communication style based on their persona:

**Bluntness (1-10)**
- Low (1-3): Diplomatic, gentle feedback
- Medium (4-7): Direct but fair
- High (8-10): Very blunt, no sugarcoating

**Humor (1-10)**
- Low (1-3): Serious, formal tone
- Medium (4-7): Occasional light humor
- High (8-10): Frequent jokes and playful banter

**Jargon Level**
- Low: Plain language, minimal tech terms
- Medium: Some industry terminology
- High: Heavy use of VC and tech jargon

### Reading Between the Lines

**Positive Signals:**
- "Tell me more about..."
- "That's interesting..."
- "Walk me through..."
- Questions about team and execution
- Discussion of next steps

**Neutral Signals:**
- Basic clarifying questions
- Requests for data and metrics
- Probing about market size
- Asking about competition

**Negative Signals:**
- "I don't see how..."
- "The market is too crowded..."
- "Your burn rate concerns me..."
- Short, dismissive responses
- Ending conversation early

### What Investors Look For

**Key Questions You'll Face:**

1. **Problem & Solution**
   - What problem are you solving?
   - Why does your solution work better?
   - Who has this problem?

2. **Market Opportunity**
   - How big is the market?
   - Who are your competitors?
   - Why now? Why you?

3. **Traction**
   - What's your current growth?
   - Revenue or user numbers?
   - Customer acquisition cost?

4. **Business Model**
   - How do you make money?
   - What are your unit economics?
   - Path to profitability?

5. **Team**
   - Why is your team uniquely qualified?
   - Previous experience?
   - Key hires planned?

6. **Ask**
   - How much are you raising?
   - What's the valuation?
   - Use of funds?

## Game Mechanics

### Turn-Based Conversations

**Turn Limit:**
- Each pitch session has a maximum of 15 turns
- One turn = your message + investor's response
- Turn counter shown at top of chat

**Why Turn Limits?**
- Simulates real pitch meetings (15-30 minutes)
- Encourages concise, focused communication
- Creates decision points (like real pitches)

### Win/Loss Outcomes

**You Win When:**
- Investor expresses strong interest
- Discusses terms and next steps
- Requests detailed follow-up materials
- Positive sentiment throughout conversation

**You Lose When:**
- Investor clearly passes on opportunity
- Major concerns not addressed
- Conversation ends negatively
- Run out of turns without closure

**Neutral Outcome:**
- "Maybe" or "Let me think about it"
- Need more information
- Come back with more traction

### Game Over Modal

After reaching turn limit or natural conversation end:

**Modal Shows:**
- Outcome (Win/Loss/Neutral)
- Key feedback points
- Areas for improvement

**Options:**
- **Restart** - Try again with same investor
- **Exit** - Return to investor selection
- **View Transcript** - Review conversation

## Tips for Success

### Preparation

**Before Starting:**
1. Know your numbers (revenue, users, growth rate)
2. Have clear problem statement
3. Understand your competition
4. Know your ask (funding amount, use of funds)
5. Practice your elevator pitch

### During the Pitch

**Do:**
- Answer questions directly
- Provide specific numbers
- Show enthusiasm but stay professional
- Acknowledge valid concerns
- Ask clarifying questions if needed

**Don't:**
- Give vague or evasive answers
- Over-exaggerate claims
- Dismiss concerns without addressing them
- Ramble or go off-topic
- Get defensive

### Handling Tough Questions

**"Your market is too crowded"**
- Acknowledge competition
- Explain your differentiation
- Show specific customer acquisition strategy

**"Your valuation is too high"**
- Be prepared to justify
- Provide comparable companies
- Show traction that supports valuation

**"What about [competitor X]?"**
- Know your competitors well
- Explain key differences
- Focus on your strengths, not their weaknesses

**"How do you plan to scale?"**
- Clear go-to-market strategy
- Hiring plan
- Technology scalability
- Customer success approach

### Practice Strategies

1. **Start with easier investors** (The Mentor is usually most forgiving)
2. **Record key feedback** from each session
3. **Iterate on your pitch** based on feedback
4. **Try different approaches** with different investors
5. **Practice specific weaknesses** (e.g., financial projections)

## Troubleshooting

### Common Issues

**"I can't see the investor list"**
- Ensure you're signed in
- Try refreshing the page
- Clear browser cache
- Check internet connection

**"Messages aren't sending"**
- Check internet connection
- Verify you haven't exceeded turn limit
- Refresh the page
- Try different browser

**"Voice input isn't working"**
- Check browser supports Web Speech API (Chrome, Edge)
- Allow microphone permissions
- Check if microphone is working in other apps
- Try incognito/private mode
- Use text input as backup

**"AI responses are slow"**
- Normal response time: 2-10 seconds
- Slow internet can increase delay
- High platform usage may cause delays
- Try again if no response after 30 seconds

**"The investor's responses seem off"**
- AI can occasionally be inconsistent
- Restart conversation if responses are problematic
- Report issue if problem persists

**"I got logged out"**
- Sessions expire after inactivity
- Simply sign in again
- Your conversation history may not persist

## FAQ

**Q: How many pitch sessions can I have?**
A: Unlimited. Practice as much as you want!

**Q: Are conversations saved?**
A: Currently, conversations are not saved. Each session is fresh.

**Q: Can I pitch to the same investor multiple times?**
A: Yes! Try different approaches and learn from each attempt.

**Q: Do investors remember previous conversations?**
A: No, each session is independent with no memory of past interactions.

**Q: What AI model is used?**
A: We use GPT-4 (OpenAI) or DeepSeek, depending on configuration.

**Q: Is my pitch data private?**
A: Yes, but note that data is processed by AI providers. Don't share highly sensitive information.

**Q: Can I customize investor personas?**
A: Only administrators can modify investor personas. Request new personas via feedback.

**Q: Is there a mobile app?**
A: No dedicated app, but the web app is mobile-responsive.

**Q: What if I run out of turns?**
A: You'll see the game over modal. You can restart and try again with a better strategy.

**Q: How realistic are the investors?**
A: Very realistic! Based on real VC communication patterns and investment theses.

**Q: Can investors actually invest in my company?**
A: No, this is a practice platform. Investors are AI simulations, not real people.

**Q: How should I prepare my first pitch?**
A: Have your key metrics ready, know your problem/solution, and start with The Mentor for easier practice.

**Q: What's the best way to improve?**
A: Practice regularly, study investor feedback patterns, iterate on weak points, and research real VC pitches.

**Q: Can I share my pitch transcript?**
A: Currently no export feature. You may screenshot or manually copy conversations.
